Hello, I’m Dustin, a Sr. AI/ML Research Scientist at Leidos.

Mount Rainier National Park, 2025

Drawing from my physics background, I specialize in physics-informed machine learning and scientific ML techniques. I also work extensively with traditional ML architectures—CNNs, Transformers, VAEs—adapting my approach to fit the problem at hand.

I work at the Leidos Innovation Center (LInC), where I focus on applied research contracts with DARPA, IARPA, and other agencies. My current work centers on space-related challenges in Space Domain Awareness and Space Situational Awareness (SDA/SSA).

I earned my PhD in Physics from The Ohio State University, where I worked with Todd Thompson investigating the physics and phenomenology of galactic starburst outflows through computational and analytic methods.

I’m based remotely in Seattle, WA and hold an active U.S. Secret Clearance. Feel free to connect with me on LinkedIn or reach out via email at (dnguyen.phys@gmail.com).
